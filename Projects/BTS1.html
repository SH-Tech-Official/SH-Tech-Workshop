First appearance and global success

BTS debuted on June 13, 2013 under Big Hit Music (now Hypebeast) with the single "No More Dream". Their success was further accelerated by the triple album The Most Beautiful Moment in Life in 2015, and they became a phenomenon in Korea, winning their first major award (Daesang) for "Album of the Year" at the Melon Music Awards in 2016. Their international breakthrough came with the song "DNA" entering the US Billboard Hot 100 in 2017.


Unique achievements

The group became the first Korean artist to top the US Billboard Hot 100 with the song "Dynamite" in 2020, and followed this success with subsequent singles such as "Butter" and "Permission to Dance". BTS is the most decorated K-pop artist, having won over 400 prestigious awards (including over 20 Grand Prizes or Daesangs at an awards ceremony). They have been appointed by the Korean government as "Special Presidential Ambassadors for Future Generations and Culture" and have received the Medal of Culture. BTS's commercial and cultural success has revolutionized the Korean entertainment industry and set new standards for global success.
